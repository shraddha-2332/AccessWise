# AccessWise Demo Script for Judges

**Duration**: 3-5 minutes  
**Goal**: Show a unique, high-impact frontend product with clear inclusion value

---

## Opening

> "Hi, I'm Shraddha, and this is **AccessWise** - an inclusive service audit platform for essential digital services.
>
> Many scholarship portals, healthcare systems, job applications, and civic websites accidentally block users through confusing instructions, language barriers, deadline pressure, or inaccessible assumptions.
>
> AccessWise helps teams catch those barriers before launch. Let me show you."

---

## Live Demo

### 1. Show homepage

Point out:
- the product branding
- service-track selector
- audit workspace
- history and guidance panels

Say:
> "This is a web application, not just a static site. Teams can audit real service flows and get a launch decision instantly."

### 2. Choose a strong example

Use the `Scholarship portal` sample or paste:

```text
Only top students with perfect English and personal laptops should apply. Upload every certificate in one session before midnight or your request will be rejected automatically. Do not contact support for exceptions.
```

Then click:
`Run inclusive audit`

### 3. Walk through the results

Say:
> "AccessWise gives a decision first, because teams need to know whether the service is safe to launch."

Highlight:
- `Block before launch`
- risk score
- number of findings
- analyzer

Then explain:
- `Deadline pressure`
- `Accessibility support`
- `Documentation burden`
- `Language inclusion`

Use language like:
> "This is not just saying something is bad. It explains exactly why users may get blocked and what should change."

### 4. Show stakeholder impacts

Say:
> "This panel makes the impact tangible. It shows what this means for users, trust, and operations. That makes the audit useful for real teams, not just for developers."

### 5. Show the action plan and rewrite

Say:
> "Now the team knows what to fix. They can rewrite the service copy, add support paths, and reduce friction before launch."

Show:
- action plan
- audit checklist
- suggested inclusive rewrite
- export buttons

### 6. Show why this is different

Say:
> "Most tools only scan for compliance issues. AccessWise audits whether real people can actually complete the service."

---

## Closing

> "AccessWise helps essential digital services become clearer, more accessible, and more inclusive before users get blocked out.
>
> It combines a strong frontend experience with real-world impact, structured explainability, and a product idea that can scale across education, healthcare, jobs, and civic platforms.
>
> Thank you."

---

## Q&A Backup

**Q: Is this only for accessibility?**  
A: "Accessibility is one part of it. We also audit clarity, language inclusion, deadline pressure, documentation burden, and dignity in service communication."

**Q: Why is this unique?**  
A: "Most existing tools are generic accessibility checkers. AccessWise is focused on essential service flows and whether real people can complete them successfully."

**Q: Why is this useful in the real world?**  
A: "Because exclusion in scholarship, healthcare, jobs, and civic services has real consequences. This helps teams find and fix those barriers early."

**Q: Does it depend on paid AI APIs?**  
A: "No. The current version is deterministic and reliable for demos, with a clear path to future AI-assisted explanation layers."
